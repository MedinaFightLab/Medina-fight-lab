Periodization for Fighters: Off-Camp vs. Fight Camp Training

Dylan Medina | Medina Fight Lab

Periodization is the science of organizing training stress over time to peak performance at the right moment. For combat athletes, the structure of your year — and how you transition between off-camp and fight camp — determines whether you show up to fight night sharp or burnt out.

Off-Camp: The Foundation Phase

The off-season is not rest. It is construction. Without a fight date forcing your timeline, you have the luxury of building qualities that can't be developed during fight camp: raw strength, hypertrophy, aerobic base, and movement competency.

Block 1 — Hypertrophy (4–6 weeks)

Higher volume, moderate intensity. 3–5 sets of 8–12 reps on compound lifts. The goal is muscle mass and connective tissue adaptation. Don't neglect single-leg and rotational work — combat sports are asymmetrical by nature.

Block 2 — Strength (4–6 weeks)

Drop volume, increase intensity. 4–5 sets of 3–6 reps. Squat, hip hinge, horizontal and vertical press/pull. Build the force production base you'll later convert to power.

Block 3 — Power Conversion (3–4 weeks)

Olympic lift derivatives (hang cleans, trap bar jumps), medicine ball work, plyometrics. You're translating strength into explosive output — the quality that directly transfers to punching power, takedown explosiveness, and kicking force.

Fight Camp: The Expression Phase

Fight camp is not the time to build fitness — it's the time to express it. Training volume in the gym must drop as technical and sparring volume rise. This is where most fighters fail: they try to maintain off-season lifting while adding two-a-day fight camp sessions. The result is overtraining, injury, and a flat performance on fight night.

Week 1–4 (Camp Entry)

Maintain strength with 2x/week lifting. Reduce volume by 30%. Emphasize power maintenance — jumps, medicine ball, speed work.

Week 5–8 (Camp Intensification)

Reduce lifting to 1–2x/week. Shift to neural activation work — short, intense, low volume. Prioritize recovery between sessions.

Week 9–10 (Peak Week)

Minimal lifting. Activation only — no fatigue. CNS is primed, not taxed. Sleep, nutrition, and sharpening technical work take priority.

The Key Principle

Fitness is built in the off-season. Fight camp reveals it. Plan accordingly.
